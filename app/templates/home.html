Reef Health Indicators

Corals are the basic building blocks of reefs, providing habitat for a diverse range of underwater organisms, and ensuring a healthy ecosystem. Tall, healthy, and abundant coral is a good indication that a reef is healthy.
Fish are vital to a stable reef because they help feed on algae and other organisms that can be harmful to corals. Many fishes also rely on coral reefs for habitat, so a healthy reef should normally have abundant fish populations.
Benthos refers to the small organisms like algae, starfish, and bacteria, that inhabit reef structures and the ocean floor. A reef’s benthic community is made up of promoters that help promote coral growth and detractors that prevent corals from surviving.
