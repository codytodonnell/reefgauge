Reef Health Indicators

Corals

are the basic building blocks of reefs, providing habitat for a diverse range of underwater organisms, and ensuring a healthy ecosystem. Tall, healthy, and abundant coral is a good indication that a reef is healthy.

Fish

are vital to a stable reef because they help feed on algae and other organisms that can be harmful to corals. Many fishes also rely on coral reefs for habitat, so a healthy reef should normally have abundant fish populations.

Benthos

refers to the small organisms like algae, starfish, and bacteria, that inhabit reef structures and the ocean floor. A reef’s benthic community is made up of promoters that help promote coral growth and detractors that prevent corals from surviving.

Communities

The scientific community

is deeply committed to understanding more about coral reefs and how to protect them. ReefGauge uses data collected by researchers through the Atlantic and Gulf Rapid Reef Assessment (AGRRA). These data measure over 700 reef sites across the Caribbean and Western Atlantic.

The diving community

has a window into coral reef environments that lets them see for themsleves what's going on underwater. ReefGauge pulls in photo observations from our citizen science project on iNaturalist to contextualize the measurements from the scientific community.
