Contribute Your Observations

ReefGauge fuels its community data through a partnering project on the non-profit citizen science platform iNaturalist. Be a part of the solution, contextualize the science, and help the community see what's going on underwater.

Getting Started

  1. Set up an account on iNaturalist. It's free, easy, and completely not-for-profit.
  2. Check out and join the ReefGauge iNaturalist project page.
  3. Start uploading your observations! If your observation fits the criteria laid out on the ReefGauge project page, then it will automatically be added to the project.

What kinds of observations can you contribute?

ReefGauge is focused on observations that help divers and scientists understand how healthy a reef is. This means that your observation should be a clear picture of coral, an herbivorous fish, a piscivorous fish, or a benthic organism like macroalgae or crustose coralline algae. Check out the different indicators and observation filters on the Explore page to see all the accepted groups species.

At the moment, ReefGauge is focused on data and observations for the Caribbean, Gulf of Mexico, and Western Atlantic. Make sure your observations fall within this geographic range.

When do new observations from iNaturalist show up in ReefGauge?

Right now, new observations from iNaturalist are pulled in bulk periodically but not instantaneously. It may take several days for your observation to show up in ReefGauge. However, ReefGauge is a new and developing tool so this may change in the future.
